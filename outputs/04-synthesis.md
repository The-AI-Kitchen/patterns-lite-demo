# Cross-lens synthesis

## Tensions

Several participants describe a tool or habit as *working* while the same passage registers sharp negative affect, which only shows up when pain and emotion are read together. P1 routes everything through Apple Reminders because it is the app they actually open, yet the morning experience is framed as dread: `[p01: "Uh. Anxious? Honestly. Like there's always more"]` (02-emotional-language.md) sits alongside the structural complaint that the morning sweep across Camino, Slack, and email routinely runs long: `[p01: "Like fifteen minutes? Sometimes longer."]` (01-pain-points.md). The causal lens adds that this stack hardened after a missed Camino email: `[p01: "So now I check Camino itself every morning before class."]` (03-causal-chains.md)—so the habit *reduces* one failure mode while the affective cost of the consolidated list remains high.

P7 is a second instructive split: the pain lens records ongoing translation labor from lab channels into Notion: `[p07: "The translation is annoying but I've done it long"]` (01-pain-points.md), while the emotion lens documents pride and even smugness about the same system: `[p07: "Honestly? Proud. Smug, even. I will admit"]` (02-emotional-language.md). The causal lens partly resolves the tension by showing the Sunday intake framed as planning time, not pure tax: `[p07: "the manual step is also when I plan, so it's"]` (03-causal-chains.md)—yet the *annoying* label and the *proud* label still coexist on the same workflow.

P4’s sticky wall illustrates valence swinging on one surface. The same artifact produces a “small thrill” when tasks complete: `[p04: "When something's done I rip it off. There's a"]` (02-emotional-language.md) and, in another week, sleep loss when load spikes: `[p04: "Last week I had nine stickies and I couldn't"]` (01-pain-points.md / 02-emotional-language.md). Pain and emotion agree the wall is heavy; the causal chain still treats the sticky as authoritative truth once copied from Camino: `[p04: "Once I see a deadline I write it on a sticky"]` (03-causal-chains.md), so the tension is not distrust of the workaround but *variance in embodied cost* week to week.

P5 is nearly the inverse: a serious near-miss is narrated as frighteningly brief affect (`[p05: "That was scary for like ten minutes."]`, 02-emotional-language.md) while the missed-cutoff pain is classified as blocker-grade: `[p05: "I forgot my Linear set was due Friday at 2pm"]` (01-pain-points.md). The new Thursday-night check habit: `[p05: "I now check Linear submission time on Thursday nights"]` (03-causal-chains.md) shows behavioral correction without sustained anxious self-description.

## Convergences

Across all three lenses, **Notion** repeatedly appears as a high-setup or high-friction tool that participants abandon or refuse despite cultural hype. Pain entries capture unused databases and organizational overhead: `[p01: "I tried Notion last quarter and it was,"]` and `[p04: "The problem with Notion is it asks me to think"]` (01-pain-points.md). Emotional language adds metaphors of emptiness or slowness: `[p04: "I made a whole reading log and then I wrote"]` (02-emotional-language.md); `[p08: "I tried Notion in like 2023. Too slow."]` (02-emotional-language.md). Causal chains spell out abandonment or switching: `[p01: "I spent like two hours setting up this beautiful"]` (03-causal-chains.md); `[p04: "The problem with Notion is it asks me to think"]` (03-causal-chains.md); `[p06: "Used it for two weeks. The thing is, Notion is"]` (03-causal-chains.md).

A second convergence is **distrust of Camino notifications paired with deliberate, scheduled extraction**. Pain lists unreliable or mistimed channels: `[p03: "They come at random hours and they don't tell"]` (01-pain-points.md); `[p04: "They came at midnight, they came on Sunday morning,"]` (01-pain-points.md). Emotion records past stress from those notifications: `[p07: "they just stressed me out without giving me actionable"]` (02-emotional-language.md). Causal accounts show the resulting behavior—turning noise off and owning timing: `[p03: "I have them turned off. They come at random hours"]` (03-causal-chains.md); `[p04: "I'd rather check Camino myself when I'm ready."]` (03-causal-chains.md).

A third convergence is **manual bridging from class or group channels into a personal system**. P1’s Slack scroll problem: `[p01: "Those deadlines live in Slack, in our group"]` (01-pain-points.md), the annoyance in retyping: `[p01: "Annoying. Like, the information is right there"]` (02-emotional-language.md), and the explicit workaround chain: `[p01: "So if I don't write them down they disappear."]` (03-causal-chains.md) line up cleanly. P7’s lab translation shows the same triad shape with a different tool at the center: `[p07: "The translation is annoying but I've done it long"]` (01-pain-points.md); `[p07: "The translation is annoying but I've done it long"]` (02-emotional-language.md); the Sunday intake chain anchors planning: `[p07: "the manual step is also when I plan, so it's"]` (03-causal-chains.md).

## Hypotheses for further inquiry

**Hypothesis 1:** Students stabilize on the capture surface with the lowest *activation energy* (opens per day), even when that surface amplifies anxiety. **Evidence:** P1’s Reminders openness sits in the emotional/tonal section as `[p01: "Reminders is dumb but I open it."]` (02-emotional-language.md) alongside morning dread `[p01: "Uh. Anxious? Honestly. Like there's always more"]` (02-emotional-language.md), while the causal lens shows retreat to Reminders after sync failure: `[p01: "So I went back to just Reminders."]` (03-causal-chains.md). **Counter-evidence:** P2 explicitly chose paper to *reduce* buzzing stress: `[p02: "So I stopped relying on the phone for school and"]` (03-causal-chains.md), so affect sometimes drives away from the “easiest” digital default rather than toward it.

**Hypothesis 2:** Calendar buffers and cross-check rituals emerge primarily *after* acute calendar collisions, not from gradual annoyance. **Evidence:** P3’s double miss and subsequent buffer rule (`[p03: "I missed both deadlines, kind of. I had to"]` in 01; `[p03: "After that I built the buffer system."]` in 03; calm/trust language in 02). P6’s Trello–sheet mismatch leading to Sunday cross-check: `[p06: "I had it on Trello but forgot to put it in"]` (01); `[p06: "Now I do a Sunday cross-check between Trello and"]` (03). **Counter-evidence:** P2’s Wednesday re-check reads as incremental adaptation to mid-week adds: `[p02: "I've started checking Camino again on Wednesday nights"]` (03) without a single named catastrophic miss in that chain entry.

**Hypothesis 3:** “Serious tool” adoption (Asana, GitHub Projects, Trello) correlates with manual calendar bridging when integrations are perceived as brittle. **Evidence:** P3 manual Asana pull: `[p03: "I pull the Asana dates into my Google Calendar"]` (01 and 03); P8 manual Camino pull and scripting: `[p08: "I pull deadlines into GitHub Projects manually every"]` (01). **Counter-evidence:** P7 loves Notion despite wanting an API: `[p07: "A Camino API. I'd pull everything into Notion"]` appears in transcript for desires, but current behavior remains weekly manual intake—integration desire does not always predict immediate workaround intensity.

**Hypothesis 4:** Transfer students’ improvised email-based tracking is emotionally costly and fragile under load. **Evidence:** Pain of missed items: `[p09: "Last week I had three things due that I didn't"]` (01); emotion cluster: `[p09: "Tired. Behind. A little embarrassed."]` (02); causal fragility of the self-email chain in 03 uses the same anchor. **Counter-evidence:** Would need follow-up interviews after first full year at SCU to see whether habits converge toward senior-style buffers without intervention.

**Hypothesis 5:** Accommodations-dependent workflows split affect: pride in a self-built pipeline coexists with exhaustion. **Evidence:** P10 pain on exhaustion: `[p10: "By Friday I'm done."]` (01); pride: `[p10: "Proud, mostly. I built this."]` (02); voice-memo chain: `[p10: "I record a voice memo every time a prof says something"]` (03). **Counter-evidence:** If weekly auto-summaries became ubiquitous, we would expect reduced *manual* audio labor; absence of that counterfactual keeps this hypothesis prospective.
