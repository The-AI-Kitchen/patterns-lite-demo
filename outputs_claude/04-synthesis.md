## Hypotheses for further inquiry

- **H1. Dependence on the income predicts negative affect more than working conditions do.** Evidence: A1 (homeless, sole income) and A11 (post-pay-cut) show the densest negative emotional language. A10 treats Flex as extra and reports the highest satisfaction ("It's fun. It's an easy way to make extra money." [A10: "It's fun. It's an easy way to make extra money."]). Counter-evidence: A6 also depends on the income but reports peak positive affect.
- **H2. In this corpus, painful incidents produce Coping or Abandonment chains, not Habit formation.** Evidence: almost all habit-formation chains are pay-optimization (A2, A3, A6, A10), not safety responses. A5's slip-on-ice accident led to abandonment, not a new safety habit. Compare to the synthetic SCU corpus where a single miss reliably installed a new check. Counter-evidence: A4's photograph-on-no-answer habit is a defensive habit installed against dispute risk.
- **H3. Comfort with self-driving cars tracks job satisfaction, not exposure to algorithmic management.** Evidence: A6, A9, A10 (job-satisfied) are open to or curious about self-driving; A7, A11 (job-disenchanted) are hostile ("I feel uncomfortable with self driving cars." [A11: "I feel uncomfortable with self driving cars."]). A11 had the deepest exposure to algorithmic management yet rejects AVs. Counter-evidence: A5, also disenchanted, does not voice strong AV opposition.
- **H4. Workers route around brokenness by building parallel infrastructure they own.** Evidence: A7's phone list, A10's mileage log and Facebook-group research, A11's post-its and notes-to-future-drivers, A6's hand-sorting before driving. Causal Pattern 5 documents this. Counter-evidence: A1 routes around brokenness by withdrawal ("I don't talk to nobody. Because you can get fired." [A1: "I don't talk to nobody. Because you can get fired."]) rather than by building.
