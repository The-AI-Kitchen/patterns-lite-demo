# Synthesis: Cross-Lens Patterns

You have access to four files in `@outputs/`:

- `01-pain-points.md`: friction and broken expectations.
- `02-emotional-language.md`: emotional valence and tonal shifts.
- `03-causal-chains.md`: trigger-action-result sequences.
- `verification.md`: which citations passed grounding checks and which did not.

## Mandatory first step

Read `verification.md` before anything else. Build a set of discarded citations: every entry classified as NOT_FOUND, MISATTRIBUTED, or UNCITED.

Any time you reference a quote in this synthesis, that quote and its citation must appear in the VERIFIED set in `verification.md`. If you cite a quote that was discarded, your synthesis is invalid.

FUZZY_MATCH citations are allowed only if you replace the snippet with the actual source text reported by the verifier, and you note the correction.

## Your job

Read the three lens outputs. Surface patterns that only become visible when the lenses are combined. Do not re-summarize any single lens.

## What to produce

### Tensions

Places where the lenses point in different directions for the same participant or situation. Example: pain point is mild, but emotional language is intense. Or: a habit formed despite a known pain point. Verified quote anchors required.

### Convergences

Places where all three lenses agree about the same situation. Strongest signals. Verified quote anchors required.

### Hypotheses for further inquiry

Three to five candidate hypotheses, each anchored in evidence from at least two lenses. Format each as:

- Hypothesis (one sentence).
- Evidence (which lens outputs and verified quotes support it).
- Counter-evidence (what would disconfirm it).

### Grounding note

End with a short paragraph stating:

- How many citations the verifier checked.
- How many were VERIFIED, FUZZY_MATCH, NOT_FOUND, MISATTRIBUTED, UNCITED.
- How many discarded quotes you had to route around.

## Format

Four sections in the order above. Tight prose, not bullet salad. Keep the whole output under 800 words.

## Out of scope

- Solution design.
- Restating the lens outputs.
- Inventing new claims not present in the lens outputs.
- Citing any quote that was not VERIFIED by the verifier.
