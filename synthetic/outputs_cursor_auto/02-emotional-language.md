## p01-cs-junior.md

1. **[p01: "I tried Notion last quarter and it was, I spent like two"]** — *"I tried Notion last quarter and it was, I spent like two hours setting up this beautiful database and then I never opened it again."* — **Valence:** mixed (positive setup → abandonment). **Object:** Notion as a tracking tool. **Intensity:** shifts mid-thought from pride in the setup to non-use.

2. **[p01: "Reminders is dumb but I open it. I open it like ten"]** — *"Reminders is dumb but I open it. I open it like ten times a day."* — **Valence:** mixed (slight put-down of the tool + reliance). **Object:** Apple Reminders.

3. **[p01: "Uh. Anxious? Honestly. Like there's always more than I remember."]** — *"Uh. Anxious? Honestly. Like there's always more than I remember."* — **Valence:** negative (with hedged opening *Uh*, *Honestly*). **Object:** opening Reminders in the morning / remembered workload.

4. **[p01: "The little red number is, yeah,"]** — *"The little red number is, yeah, I have a love-hate with that number."* — **Valence:** mixed. **Object:** the badge count on Reminders. **Intensity:** *yeah* softens before naming *love-hate*.

5. **[p01: "Annoying. Like, the information is right there in Slack. Why"]** — *"Annoying. Like, the information is right there in Slack. Why am I retyping it into another app."* — **Valence:** negative. **Object:** manually copying Slack deadlines into Reminders.

2. **[p03: "I had to ask my prof for an extension and explain to my"]** — *"I had to ask my prof for an extension and explain to my manager why my deck wasn't ready. I felt sick the whole week."* — **Valence:** negative. **Object:** the week after missing both deadlines.

4. **[p03: "The notifications are a joke. I have them turned off. They come"]** — *"The notifications are a joke. I have them turned off. They come at random hours and they don't tell me if something is actually urgent."* — **Valence:** negative / dismissive. **Object:** Camino notifications.

5. **[p03: "The filter helps but it shouldn't be necessary."]** — *"The filter helps but it shouldn't be necessary."* — **Valence:** mixed (gratitude + frustration). **Object:** Gmail filter for one professor’s emails.

## p04-english-junior.md

1. **[p04: "When something's done I rip it off. There's a small thrill"]** — *"When something's done I rip it off. There's a small thrill in ripping it off."* — **Valence:** positive. **Object:** completing a task (sticky note ritual).

2. **[p04: "It depends on the week. Some weeks the wall is calm and"]** — *"It depends on the week. Some weeks the wall is calm and I feel powerful. Some weeks the wall is, you know, a wall. I sit at my desk and I just feel the weight of it. Last week I had nine stickies and I couldn't sleep."* — **Valence:** mixed across clauses (powerful → weighed down → sleep loss). **Object:** the sticky-note wall. **Intensity:** sharp flip from *powerful* to embodied *weight* and *couldn't sleep* within one answer.

3. **[p04: "Notion. Of course Notion. Everyone"]** — *"Notion. Of course Notion. Everyone tries Notion. I made a whole reading log and then I wrote in it three times. It's a graveyard."* — **Valence:** negative metaphor. **Object:** abandoned Notion reading log.

4. **[p04: "The problem with Notion is it asks me to think about how"]** — *"The problem with Notion is it asks me to think about how to organize. The wall doesn't ask. The wall just is."* — **Valence:** relief / acceptance (contrasted with Notion as demanding). **Object:** wall vs Notion’s cognitive load.

5. **[p04: "I have them off. They came at midnight, they came on Sunday morning,"]** — *"I have them off. They came at midnight, they came on Sunday morning, they didn't respect any kind of human rhythm. I'd rather check Camino myself when I'm ready."* — **Valence:** negative moralizing (*didn't respect*). **Object:** Camino notification timing.

## p05-math-sophomore.md

1. **[p05: "I just remember. Honestly. I"]** — *"I just remember. Honestly. I know that sounds dumb."* — **Valence:** hedged negative self-judgment (*sounds dumb*). **Object:** mnemonic-only system.

2. **[p05: "If a prof adds something unusual,"]** — *"If a prof adds something unusual, I'll write it on the back of my hand. Like literally on my hand. Sounds insane but it works."* — **Valence:** hedged (*Sounds insane*) with confidence (*works*). **Object:** handwriting deadlines on skin.

3. **[p05: "No, mostly not. I had"]** — *"No, mostly not. I had a moment two weeks ago when I forgot my Linear set was due Friday at 2pm instead of midnight and I almost missed it. That was scary for like ten minutes."* — **Valence:** hedged calm → brief negative spike. **Object:** almost missing a Linear deadline. **Intensity:** *scary for like ten minutes* tightens then releases.

4. **[p05: "Sometimes. Especially when other"]** — *"Sometimes. Especially when other people talk about their planners and apps and color codes. I wonder if I'm getting away with something."* — **Valence:** hedged unease. **Object:** comparing own minimal system to peers’ tools.

## p10-marketing-senior.md

1. **[p10: "Camino is hard to use"]** — *"Camino is hard to use for me. The text is dense, the layout changes from class to class. I can do it, it just takes a while."* — **Valence:** negative struggle, then resilient hedge (*I can do it*). **Object:** reading Camino with dyslexia-related friction.

2. **[p10: "It works, but it's exhausting."]** — *"It works, but it's exhausting. I'm essentially doing the deadline tracking by hand, in audio, every week. By Friday I'm done."* — **Valence:** mixed (*works* / *exhausting* / depletion by Friday). **Object:** voice-memo-to-Reminders workflow.

3. **[p10: "I have extended time on"]** — *"I have extended time on exams and a couple of professors send me weekly summaries of what's due, which is huge. The summary is a paragraph and I can take my time with it."* — **Valence:** strongly positive (*huge*). **Object:** professor-provided weekly summaries.

4. **[p10: "No, two professors do it."]** — *"No, two professors do it. The other two, I have to ask, and asking is the part I hate. I shouldn't have to ask every quarter. It's awkward."* — **Valence:** negative (*hate*, *awkward*). **Object:** having to request accommodations-style summaries each quarter.

5. **[p10: "I tried Notion. The blinking"]** — *"I tried Notion. The blinking cursor and the empty page made me anxious. Reminders is better because it's small. One line per item. I can handle that."* — **Valence:** negative → relief. **Object:** Notion blank page vs Reminders’ simplicity.

6. **[p10: "Proud, mostly. I built this."]** — *"Proud, mostly. I built this. Other students see it and think it's weird. From the outside it looks weird. From the inside it makes sense."* — **Valence:** mixed / positive core with social sting (*weird* twice). **Object:** personal voice-memo workflow and others’ perceptions.

7. **[p10: "Auto-summaries from Camino. If"]** — *"Auto-summaries from Camino. If Camino itself sent me a weekly paragraph saying "here is what is due in your four classes this week," that would change my life."* — **Valence:** strongly positive (hyperbolic hope). **Object:** hypothetical Camino auto-summary feature.
