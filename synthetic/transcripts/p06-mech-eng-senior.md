# Transcript p06 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P6, Mechanical Engineering senior, three classes plus capstone team
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: Walk me through how you keep track of your week.

P6: Two systems. Capstone is in Trello because that's what my team voted for in September. My personal stuff is in a Google Sheet I built sophomore year. The sheet has columns for class, assignment, due date, status, and time estimate. It works.

I: Why a spreadsheet?

P6: Because I can see the whole week at once. Apps make you scroll. The sheet is one screen. I have it pinned in a browser tab and it's open most of the day.

I: How does Camino fit in?

P6: I check Camino for each class once a week, on Sunday, and update the sheet. I don't trust Camino as my source. I trust the sheet. The sheet is what I built. It's mine.

I: Tell me about Trello for capstone.

P6: We have lists for backlog, this week, in review, done. Each card has a due date and an assignee. It works because the team trusts it. The faculty advisor doesn't look at Trello so we also have to send him a weekly status email which is its own task. He wants a paragraph. We rotate who writes it.

I: How does it feel managing two systems?

P6: Mostly fine. Sometimes I do double entry, like a Trello deadline that also affects my personal week, but I just live with it. The bigger annoyance is when Trello and the spreadsheet disagree about when something's due, and I have to figure out which one is wrong.

I: Has anything fallen through recently?

P6: A subsystem review last month. I had it on Trello but forgot to put it in my spreadsheet. I scheduled an exam study block on top of it. I had to apologize to the team and reschedule my study. Now I do a Sunday cross-check between Trello and the sheet.

I: Have you tried Notion?

P6: I tried Notion. Made a beautiful page. Used it for two weeks. The thing is, Notion is for thinking about your system, and my system is already built. I don't want to rebuild every quarter.

I: What about Camino notifications?

P6: Off. They came at random times and I'd just snooze them. I check Camino on my schedule, not its.

I: One change?

P6: Let me export Camino assignments as a CSV. Like, one button, all assignments across all my classes, CSV. I'll do the rest. The data is already there, just let me have it.
