# Transcript p01 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P1, CS junior, four classes plus a lab
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: Walk me through how you tracked assignments last week.

P1: Last week. Okay. So I have Camino for, you know, the official stuff. That's where professors post. But not every professor uses it the same way. Like in 174 the prof posts everything on the course website, not in Camino. And in 129 the assignments page is actually pretty good but the calendar is wrong half the time? Like the due dates on the calendar and the due dates on the assignment don't match.

I: When you say don't match, can you give an example?

P1: Yeah, last week the calendar said something was due Friday and the assignment page said Wednesday. I caught it because I went to start it on Thursday and was like wait. But that's the kind of thing where if I'd trusted the calendar I would have just missed it. So I don't trust the calendar at all anymore. I use the assignment list.

I: What do you use to keep track across all four classes?

P1: Honestly? Apple Reminders. I tried Notion last quarter and it was, I spent like two hours setting up this beautiful database and then I never opened it again. Reminders is dumb but I open it. I open it like ten times a day.

I: What goes into Reminders?

P1: Anything with a deadline. So homework, but also like, my group for 129 has its own Slack and we have stuff due to each other internally. Like our teammate is supposed to send us his Figma file by Sunday. That goes in Reminders too because if I waited for him to actually do it I'd never see his contribution.

I: How do you feel when you open Reminders in the morning?

P1: Uh. Anxious? Honestly. Like there's always more than I remember. There's the thing I forgot from yesterday that rolled over, plus today's stuff. The little red number is, yeah, I have a love-hate with that number.

I: Tell me about a recent moment when something fell through the cracks.

P1: Two weeks ago. Or three? I missed a Camino post because the email came in at like 11pm and I had Do Not Disturb on. My phone was dark. I woke up, didn't check email, went to lecture, and the prof was like "as I mentioned in the post last night" and I was like, the post? So now I check Camino itself every morning before class. I don't trust email notifications.

I: That's interesting. Walk me through what you do every morning.

P1: Okay so I wake up, I look at Reminders. I open Camino in a browser tab and click through each of my four classes. I scan for new posts, new assignments. I check Slack for both my group chats. Then I check my regular email, which is mostly garbage, but sometimes something real shows up.

I: How long does that take?

P1: Like fifteen minutes? Sometimes longer. If something new dropped I might lose another ten minutes figuring out if it's due immediately or not.

I: Have you tried anything to make that faster?

P1: I tried Notion. I said that. I tried setting up an iCal feed for Camino, which exists, but it kept showing duplicate events and I couldn't figure out which one to delete. I gave up. I tried using my Google Calendar more, but then I'd put stuff in Google Calendar AND Reminders, and they'd disagree, and now I'm checking three things instead of two. So I went back to just Reminders.

I: What would the ideal version of this look like?

P1: Just one place. I want, like, when I wake up, a list. Everything I need to know. Camino posts since yesterday, anything due today or tomorrow, what my group owes me, what I owe them. Just the list. I don't need it to be smart, I just need it to be in one place.

I: You said your group owes you things. Tell me more about that.

P1: Yeah so for 129 we have five people and we have, you know, the milestone every two weeks. Internally we set our own deadlines. Like, "Send me your interview transcripts by Tuesday." And those deadlines live in Slack, in our group chat, which scrolls. So if I don't write them down they disappear. That's where Reminders helps. But it's manual. I have to translate every message into a reminder.

I: How does that feel, doing that translation work?

P1: Annoying. Like, the information is right there in Slack. Why am I retyping it into another app. But if I don't, I forget.

I: Last question. If I gave you a magic wand, no constraints, what would you change?

P1: I want Camino to just be good. Like, just put the actual due dates on the calendar and make the notifications work. That's it. The professors will still be inconsistent, that's a people problem. But the platform should be solid. Right now it's not solid and I have built this whole second system around it.
