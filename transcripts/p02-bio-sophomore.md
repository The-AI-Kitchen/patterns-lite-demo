# Transcript p02 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P2, Biology sophomore, four classes plus two lab sections
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: How do you keep track of what's due?

P2: I have a paper planner. The kind with a week on each spread. I write everything in pencil because deadlines shift. My mom got it for me at the start of fall quarter and I thought it was kind of corny but it's, I don't know, it's working.

I: Why paper?

P2: I used my phone before and the notifications stressed me out. Like, every five minutes something was buzzing. Slack, Camino, group chat, Snapchat. I couldn't tell which buzz was the important one. So I stopped relying on the phone for school and now my phone is just texts and music.

I: How does the paper planner interact with Camino?

P2: Every Sunday night I open Camino for all four classes, look at the next two weeks, and copy due dates into the planner. It takes like twenty minutes. The thing is, professors add stuff during the week, so by Wednesday my planner is wrong. I've started checking Camino again on Wednesday nights to catch the new stuff.

I: What does it feel like when you find something new mid-week?

P2: A little jolt. Like, oh no, what else is hiding in there. Then I copy it in pencil, sometimes I have to rearrange the week. It's annoying but I've made peace with it.

I: Tell me about a recent miss.

P2: Two Fridays ago I missed a quiz in OChem. The prof posted it Thursday night, I didn't check Camino Thursday because I had a lab until 9pm and I just crashed when I got home. Friday morning I went to lecture and other people were talking about the quiz. I had to email the prof. He let me make it up but I felt so dumb.

I: How are your group project deadlines tracked?

P2: For my Bio 161 group we have a WhatsApp. Internal deadlines live there. I write them in my planner too, in a different color. The translation is the worst part. People send three messages in a row and the deadline is buried in message two of three.

I: Have you tried any digital tools recently?

P2: I tried Google Calendar last year. I'd put due dates in and then I'd see them on my phone and I'd ignore them because everything else was on my phone too. The planner works because I have to physically open it. It's a friction in a good way.

I: One change you'd make if you could?

P2: Make Camino show me a clean weekly view across all my classes. Right now I have to click into each class. I'd pay for that view, honestly.
