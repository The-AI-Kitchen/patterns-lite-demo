# Transcript p03 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P3, Business senior, three classes plus a part-time internship at a SaaS startup
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: Talk me through your week.

P3: Google Calendar runs my life. Everything is in there. Class blocks, internship hours, study sessions, gym, even meals. Color coded. School is blue, work is green, personal is orange.

I: How do deadlines get into the calendar?

P3: Every Friday afternoon I do a planning hour. I look at Camino for the next two weeks, pull each due date into Calendar as a timed event two days before it's actually due. Two-day buffer. That's the trick. If something's due Sunday I put it in Calendar as a Friday event so I have the weekend in case anything goes wrong.

I: How did you land on that system?

P3: Last spring I had a marketing project due the same day my internship had a Tuesday all-day offsite. I missed both deadlines, kind of. I had to ask my prof for an extension and explain to my manager why my deck wasn't ready. I felt sick the whole week. After that I built the buffer system.

I: You sound calm about deadlines now.

P3: Yeah. It's not that I don't have stress, it's that the calendar absorbs the stress for me. I trust the calendar. If it's not in the calendar it doesn't exist.

I: How does Camino fit in?

P3: Camino is a source. I extract from it. I don't live in it. The notifications are a joke. I have them turned off. They come at random hours and they don't tell me if something is actually urgent.

I: Group projects?

P3: My MGMT 175 group uses Asana. We adopted it because everyone in the group has internship experience and we wanted to look serious. It actually works. Internal deadlines, dependencies, the whole thing. I pull the Asana dates into my Google Calendar manually because the integration is sketchy.

I: What's a current pain point?

P3: My professor for MGMT 175 has not updated Camino since week four. The class is run through emails. I have a Gmail filter that flags anything from her, otherwise I'd lose half the assignments. The filter helps but it shouldn't be necessary.

I: One change?

P3: Sync. I want everything I write in Camino to land in my Google Calendar automatically with my two-day buffer. The two-day buffer is mine, I get that. But the base data should flow.
