# Transcript p04 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P4, English junior, four classes plus on-campus job at the writing center
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: How do you stay on top of deadlines?

P4: Sticky notes. I have a stretch of wall above my desk with about thirty of them right now. One per task. When something's done I rip it off. There's a small thrill in ripping it off.

I: Do you use Camino?

P4: I open Camino to find the assignments, but I don't trust it as my source of truth. Once I see a deadline I write it on a sticky and the sticky is the truth. The sticky goes on the wall in due-date order. Yes I am the human implementation of a database.

I: What happens when a deadline shifts?

P4: I cross out the date and write the new one. If the sticky gets too messy I rewrite it on a fresh one. I've gone through a lot of stickies this quarter.

I: How does it feel to look at the wall?

P4: It depends on the week. Some weeks the wall is calm and I feel powerful. Some weeks the wall is, you know, a wall. I sit at my desk and I just feel the weight of it. Last week I had nine stickies and I couldn't sleep.

I: Have you tried any digital tools?

P4: Notion. Of course Notion. Everyone tries Notion. I made a whole reading log and then I wrote in it three times. It's a graveyard. The problem with Notion is it asks me to think about how to organize. The wall doesn't ask. The wall just is.

I: What about Camino notifications?

P4: I have them off. They came at midnight, they came on Sunday morning, they didn't respect any kind of human rhythm. I'd rather check Camino myself when I'm ready.

I: Group projects?

P4: I'm in a workshop class which has peer reviews due every other week. The peer review schedule is a Google Doc the prof made. I copy the date onto a sticky like everything else. Group chat for the class is Discord, which is fine, but the deadlines that matter are on the doc, not in the chat.

I: One change you'd make?

P4: Make it easier to print a clean list. Like, give me one page, one button, all my deadlines for the next month, and I'll go from there. I don't want a dashboard, I want a printable list.
