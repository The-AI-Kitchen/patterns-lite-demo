# Transcript p07 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P7, Psychology junior, four classes plus a research lab assistantship
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: How do you keep track of what's due?

P7: Notion. I know everyone hates Notion. I'm the one person it works for. I built this database over freshman and sophomore year. It survived three Notion redesigns. I love it.

I: Walk me through it.

P7: Top-level page is a calendar view. Each item has a class tag, a type tag (reading, paper, problem set, lab, exam), and a status. I can filter to just "this week, not done." That's the view I open in the morning.

I: How does Camino feed into Notion?

P7: Every Sunday I do an intake. I open Camino, look at the next two weeks per class, and create Notion items for anything new. It takes me about half an hour. I've thought about automating it but the manual step is also when I plan, so it's not wasted time.

I: How did you land on Notion?

P7: I tried Reminders, I tried a paper planner, I tried Google Calendar. The thing about Notion that worked is the database. I can sort by due date, filter by class, view as a calendar, view as a kanban. The same data, multiple views. That's the unlock.

I: How do you feel about your system?

P7: Honestly? Proud. Smug, even. I will admit that. My friends mock me for being so organized and I take it as a compliment.

I: Where does the lab work fit?

P7: The lab has its own deadlines. My PI runs everything through Slack and email. I copy her deadlines into Notion under a "Lab" tag. The translation is annoying but I've done it long enough that it's automatic.

I: Has anything fallen through recently?

P7: One time. Last spring. I had a paper draft due Friday and I marked it for Wednesday in Notion because I wanted to finish early. I then somehow thought Wednesday was the real due date and submitted a half draft. I got it back Friday with the grade and was confused. Then I realized I'd outsmarted myself. Lesson: don't lie to your own system.

I: Camino notifications?

P7: Off. I check Camino on Sunday. The notifications were not helpful, they just stressed me out without giving me actionable info.

I: One change?

P7: A Camino API. I'd pull everything into Notion automatically. The ical feed is too brittle.
