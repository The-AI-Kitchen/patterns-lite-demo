# Transcript p10 — Synthetic / Example Data

EXAMPLE DATA: Fictional interview. No real participant is represented.

Participant: P10, Marketing senior, four classes, has dyslexia and works with Accessible Education accommodations
Topic: How students track academic deadlines at SCU

---

I: How do you keep track of what's due?

P10: Voice memos and Reminders. I record a voice memo every time a prof says something important in class. Then later that day I listen back and put the deadlines into Reminders. The voice memo is the buffer between hearing something and writing it down.

I: Why voice memos?

P10: Because I read slowly. If a prof says "this is due next Friday" and I try to write it down in real time, I miss the next sentence. The voice memo lets me catch the deadline without losing the thread.

I: How does Camino fit in?

P10: Camino is hard to use for me. The text is dense, the layout changes from class to class. I can do it, it just takes a while. So I rely on my own ear in class more than Camino.

I: How does that feel?

P10: It works, but it's exhausting. I'm essentially doing the deadline tracking by hand, in audio, every week. By Friday I'm done.

I: Tell me about accommodations.

P10: I have extended time on exams and a couple of professors send me weekly summaries of what's due, which is huge. The summary is a paragraph and I can take my time with it. It saves me from having to dig through Camino.

I: Does the summary work for every class?

P10: No, two professors do it. The other two, I have to ask, and asking is the part I hate. I shouldn't have to ask every quarter. It's awkward.

I: Have you tried apps?

P10: I tried Notion. The blinking cursor and the empty page made me anxious. Reminders is better because it's small. One line per item. I can handle that.

I: Group projects?

P10: My MKTG 175 group is mostly text-based. We use iMessage. I record voice memos of our group calls when I'm allowed to, so I don't have to take notes during. Sometimes I just transcribe the audio later.

I: How does it feel to have your own workflow?

P10: Proud, mostly. I built this. Other students see it and think it's weird. From the outside it looks weird. From the inside it makes sense.

I: One change?

P10: Auto-summaries from Camino. If Camino itself sent me a weekly paragraph saying "here is what is due in your four classes this week," that would change my life. The data is there. Just write it as a paragraph.
